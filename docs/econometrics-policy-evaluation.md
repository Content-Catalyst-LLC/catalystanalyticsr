# Econometrics and Policy Evaluation

Catalyst Analytics R v1.4.0 adds governed statistical and quasi-experimental workflows for policy evaluation.

## Supported methods

- Multiple regression with classical, HC1, or clustered uncertainty
- Unit, time, and two-way fixed-effects panel models
- Difference-in-differences
- Dynamic event studies
- Interrupted time series
- Synthetic controls
- Residual, heteroskedasticity, autocorrelation, and conditioning diagnostics

## Identification governance

Every causal interpretation must name the assumptions on which it depends. Parallel trends, no anticipation, no interference, absence of concurrent shocks, convex-hull support, and functional-form choices are reviewable evidence records. Statistical significance is not treated as causal authorization.

## Publication boundary

Policy-evaluation bundles preserve estimates, uncertainty, assumptions, diagnostics, review status, and integrity records. They do not authorize a policy or replace expert review.
